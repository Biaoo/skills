# Human Intervention

Load this reference when a live design uncertainty concerns consequential human contribution, actual decision or release authority, independent review, stale approval, no-response behavior, safe states, review capacity, accessibility, correction, or appeal. Human presence alone is not a control, and this document is not an expansion ladder to traverse.

## Enter at the Human Uncertainty That Matters

Possible entry points include:

- what information, judgment, lived standpoint, or authority a human contributes;
- whether a named reviewer can actually decide the matter;
- what evidence makes review substantive rather than ceremonial;
- what happens when a person disagrees, does not respond, or lacks capacity;
- whether an inherited approval still covers the current artifact, population, or action;
- how affected people can obtain correction, appeal, restoration, or remedy.

Use only the concerns that can change the current intervention or disposition. A low-impact co-editing interaction should not acquire an enterprise governance contract merely because a person participates.

## Distinguish Local Control Relationships

- **Human-in-the-loop:** a human participates in a required local transition.
- **Human-on-the-loop:** a system operates while a human monitors and can intervene.
- **Human-in-command:** a person or institution sets automation boundaries, grants authority, and retains stopping and accountability powers.
- **Mixed initiative:** humans and Agents may both propose actions, questions, revisions, or escalation.

A project can use several relationships at different decisions. Do not select one global HITL mode.

## Make an Intervention Answerable

At the resolution needed for the decision, make clear:

- what question or contribution requires a person and why;
- the exact subject, version, population, channel, or action affected;
- the observable trigger and useful timing;
- the expertise, context, lived standpoint, or institutional role needed;
- what the person may actually advise, edit, decide, veto, accept, authorize, execute, pause, or withdraw;
- what original evidence, provenance, uncertainty, alternatives, and consequences they can inspect;
- how each allowed response, and no response, changes the route.

These are decision semantics, not seven fields that must be visibly populated. Ordinary target negotiation, sample calibration, candidate selection, co-editing, and low-impact advice need only enough clarity to prevent ambiguity. A binding or consequential intervention cannot omit its covered subject, actual authority, permitted action, material evidence, and route effect.

## Establish Authority Rather Than Inferring It

A title such as expert, teacher, owner, clinician, or reviewer does not establish authority. Where the decision is consequential, preserve the source and scope of authority, relevant competence or standpoint, delegation and revocation, conflicts of interest, separation of duties, emergency conditions, and disagreement resolver.

Keep logically distinct:

- experiential contribution;
- expert recommendation;
- policy interpretation;
- risk acceptance;
- binding decision;
- release authorization;
- action execution;
- incident and remediation responsibility.

One person may hold several non-conflicting responsibilities in a low-impact project. Combining actors does not erase the distinctions.

## Make Review Substantive and Decision-Ready

A blocking review needs an answerable question, applicable criteria, material claims or exceptions, uncertainty that may remain, evidence that closes or reopens the judgment, and conditions that require escalation or narrower scope. Opening a page, time-on-screen, a checkbox, click, or signature is not substantive completion evidence.

Provide enough context for independent judgment without letting the producer define the whole evidence boundary. Depending on the decision, useful material may include the exact artifact and diff, original sources and retrieval time, source-selection and exclusion rules, unavailable evidence, policy or rubric version, knowns and disputes, alternatives, consequences, reversibility, remedy, affected parties, permitted actions, deadline, and no-response behavior.

Label machine summaries. For consequential review, consider source-first or independent-first judgment before revealing the Agent recommendation. Use blind comparison when presentation order, polish, model identity, or ranking could anchor the reviewer.

## Separate Observation, Decision, Authorization, and Execution

Do not use a bare `approved` state to stand for several acts:

```text
review observation
non-binding recommendation
binding decision
release authorization
release execution
```

Where material, bind a decision record to actor and authority source, exact artifact or version, relevant evidence and dependency versions, policy and configuration, covered population/channel/jurisdiction, conditions and dissent, validity period, and revocation conditions.

A receipt records what was decided. It does not prove correctness and must not become a transferable authorization token. Production execution requires its own authenticated, scoped, time-bounded enforcement. Markdown cannot provide it.

## Choose Action-Specific No-Response and Safe States

There is no universal fail-open or fail-closed rule. A local safe disposition might hold an unpublished draft, freeze distribution or add a warning during active harm, use a last-known-valid or degraded service, checkpoint and pause an execution, or apply the narrowest reversible temporary restriction to a rights-affecting decision.

Where no response or failure is consequential, clarify the owner, entry signal, duration, monitoring, notification, exit and recovery evidence, fallback if containment fails, and evidence that the safe state does not create greater harm.

Escalation needs an actual recipient, response expectation, timeout action, backup, disagreement resolver, closure authority, and reopen or appeal path. If those are unavailable, narrow or block the affected automation rather than silently timing out into release.

## Protect Judgment from Automation Bias

Model confidence is not a general permission system. It may prioritize locally calibrated review, but it cannot remove hard obligations, rights controls, source-conflict handling, or difficult-to-reverse gates.

Possible countermeasures include independent-first or source-first disposition, blind or randomized comparison, counterevidence, original-source sampling, and controlled calibration with seeded defects. Select them only when they can expose a plausible mechanism.

Measure outcomes such as erroneous recommendation acceptance, defect detection with and without model exposure, judgment shift, disagreement quality, correction rate, and missed harms. Override counts or time-on-screen alone do not demonstrate effective review.

## Match Standing Review to Population and Capacity

For repeated or continuous work, a standing decision applies only to an eligible population with explicit invariants, exclusions, allowed deltas, coverage claim, expiry, invalidation, and propagation rules. Do not claim `human reviewed` without identifying the layer, population or sample, and period covered.

Risk-targeted review can be combined with random exploration to discover confident unknown failures. State the sampling unit, unreviewed scope, expansion trigger, and disposition. Sampling does not waive units that an obligation requires to be reviewed.

Before relying on a review queue, obtain local evidence about arrival rate, handling time, rework, skill and language coverage, handoffs, breaks, and surge recovery. Under overload, reduce automation scope, population, frequency, or functionality. Do not preserve throughput through bulk approval, hidden evidence, weaker sampling, or release-by-timeout.

## Make Human Work Accessible and Resourced

The control interface must permit the real person to inspect evidence and exercise their authority. Depending on the context, this can require keyboard and assistive-technology operation, text alternatives for visual information, language support, low-bandwidth or asynchronous paths, adjustable time, original-source access, and accessible reject, veto, pause, appeal, or emergency controls.

Treat review, explanation, appeal, correction, maintenance, and community liaison as funded work. Identify resource ownership, training and paid time, backup and handoff, exposure limits, rest and support, privacy and contribution rights, and non-retaliation for rejection or escalation. If essential labor is not resourced, narrow or block the design rather than calling it a later operational detail.

## Design Correction and Appeal Where Harm Is Material

An affected person may need the applicable decision and rule, a comprehensible reason and disclosable evidence, an accessible correction or appeal channel, an independent reviewer with reversal authority, temporary containment, restoration and downstream synchronization, and evidence that remedy occurred.

A rerun of the same model or self-review by the original decision maker is not independent appeal. If enforcement, moderation, or appeals are the system's primary purpose rather than part of a generation lifecycle, hand the central governance design to an appropriate dedicated method.

## Preserve Continuation and Reopen Locally

When a human decision remains unresolved, preserve the pending question, covered subject/version/population, evidence status, qualified owner and actual authority, no-response disposition, freshness or deadline, and action that remains blocked.

Reopen the affected decision when its artifact or population materially changes; authority expires or is revoked; policy, consent, or applicable evidence changes; counterevidence appears; review capacity falls below the assumed control; an appeal or observed harm challenges the disposition; or evidence shows the intervention is ineffective. Revalidate only the dependent envelope and retain the prior rationale.

Expected behavior includes:

- `expert reviewed` without scoped authority cannot authorize release;
- approval for one version does not silently cover a changed artifact;
- confidence cannot remove a statutory or rights-related gate;
- overload narrows automation rather than enabling bulk approval;
- low-impact collaboration remains proportionate.

These examples are regression probes, not a checklist for every design.

## Retire Ineffective Human Controls

A substantial intervention should have a targeted obligation or harm, plausible mechanism, minimum scope, lighter alternative, known burden, effectiveness signal, and shrink or retirement condition. Remove or narrow a control that does not reduce material misses, rework, withdrawals, appeal reversals, or harm, or whose burden exceeds its benefit. More human review is not automatically more assurance.
